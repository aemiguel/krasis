PROMPT: If it takes 4 hours for 4 towels to dry on a clothesline in the sun, how long does it take for 20 towels to dry under the exact same conditions?
RESPONSE: This is a classic trick question!

The key insight is: **drying time depends on the drying *conditions*, not the number of towels**, assuming they’re all exposed to the same conditions (sunlight, wind, humidity, etc.) and **each towel dries independently**.

If 4 towels take 4 hours to dry, that means each towel individually takes 4 hours to dry — not that the group as a whole takes longer or shorter depending on count.

So, under the **exact same conditions**, each towel still takes **4 hours** to dry.

Therefore, **20 towels** will also take **4 hours** to dry — assuming they’re all laid out on the clothesline (or lines) with enough space and airflow so that each dries at the same rate as the others.

✅ Answer: **4 hours**.

⚠️ Note: If the clothesline is too short and towels are bunched up, reducing airflow, then drying *could* take longer — but the question says “exact same conditions,” implying optimal, unchanged setup.
