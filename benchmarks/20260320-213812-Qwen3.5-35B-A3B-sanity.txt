The fundamental intuition behind the Binary Chop is **elimination**.

If the word you want comes *before* the current page, you discard the second half. If it comes *after*, you discard the first half. You repeat this until you find the word or determine it doesn't exist.

RESPONSE:

Here is the reasoning:
1.  **Parallel Drying:** When you hang towels on a clothesline, they dry simultaneously, not sequentially. The sun and air dry all the towels at the same time.
2.  **Constant Conditions:** Since the conditions (sun, wind, temperature) are the "exact same," the rate of evaporation for each towel remains constant.
3.  **The Logic:** If 4 towels take 4 hours to dry, it means the drying process takes 4 hours. Adding more towels (assuming they fit on the line without blocking each other's sunlight or airflow) does not change the time required for the sun to dry them.
